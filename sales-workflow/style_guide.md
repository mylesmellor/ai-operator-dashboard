# Sales Communication Style Guide

## Tone
- Professional, warm, and direct
- Confident but not arrogant
- Helpful without being pushy

## Rules
1. **Short paragraphs** — 2–3 sentences maximum per paragraph.
2. **Clear CTAs** — Every email must end with a specific next step (e.g., "Would Tuesday at 2pm work for a 20-minute call?").
3. **No guarantees** — Never promise specific outcomes, ROI figures, or timelines you cannot control.
4. **No hype or buzzwords** — Avoid: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "synergy", "leverage", "disrupt", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class".
5. **No filler phrases** — Remove: "I hope this email finds you well", "Just following up", "I wanted to reach out", "As per our conversation".
6. **Specificity over generality** — Reference the lead's actual situation, company, or pain points.
7. **Honest about unknowns** — If pricing, timeline, or scope is uncertain, say so clearly with "[To be confirmed]".
8. **British English** — Use: finalise, organise, colour, behaviour, etc.
9. **No exaggerated claims** — If you haven't verified a fact, don't state it as certain.
10. **Human review required** — All drafts are suggestions. A human must review and approve before sending.
